What is Ropani? (Nepali Land Unit)

Ropani is the primary land measurement unit used in hilly and mountainous regions of Nepal, including Kathmandu Valley. One Ropani equals 5,476 square feet or 508.72 square meters. It is divided into 16 Aana, 64 Paisa, and 256 Dam. Ropani is widely used for residential property measurement and government land records in hill districts.

What is Bigha? (Terai Land Unit)

Bigha is the standard land measurement unit in the Terai (plains) region of Nepal. One Bigha equals 72,900 square feet or 6,772.63 square meters. It is divided into 20 Kattha and 400 Dhur. Bigha is used extensively for agricultural land, real estate transactions, and government land registration in Terai districts like Chitwan, Rupandehi, Morang, and Sunsari.

Ropani vs Bigha — Which Region Uses What?

Nepal uses two different land measurement systems depending on the region. The hilly and mountain regions (including Kathmandu, Pokhara, and most hill districts) use the Ropani system. The Terai plains (Madhesh Province, Bagmati Province lowlands, and Lumbini Province flatlands) use the Bigha system. This converter supports both systems, allowing seamless conversion between them.
