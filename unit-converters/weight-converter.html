Nepal Gold Weight Units — Tola, Lal, Rati Explained

Nepal uses traditional South Asian gold weight units that differ from international troy weights. The primary unit is the Tola (तोला), which equals exactly 11.6638 grams. All gold jewellery pricing, buying, and selling in Nepal's jewellery markets (Thamel, Indrachowk, Mahalaxmisthan) uses Tola as the base unit.

1 Tola = 12 Lal = 96 Rati = 11.6638 grams = 0.3750 Troy Oz

How to Convert Tola to Gram?

Multiply the number of Tola by 11.6638 to get grams. For example, 2.5 Tola = 2.5 × 11.6638 = 29.16 grams. This is the standard formula used by Nepal Rastra Bank and licensed gold dealers.

Nepal Traditional Weight Units (Dharni, Pau, Mann)

For vegetables, grains, and everyday goods, Nepal markets use traditional units. The Pau (250g) and Dharni (2.4 kg) are widely used in Terai vegetable markets. The Mann (Maund, ~37.3 kg) is used for bulk agricultural trade, equivalent to 40 traditional Seers.

Nepal Grain Measurement — Muri, Pathi, Mana

Dry grain in Nepal is traditionally measured in volume units: Mana (smallest, ~0.6 kg of rice), Pathi (8 Mana, ~4.8 kg), and Muri (16 Pathi, ~76.8 kg). These are volume-based measures, so exact weight varies slightly by grain type and density.

Why Gold Prices Use Troy Ounce Internationally

International gold markets (London Bullion Market, COMEX) quote prices in Troy Ounces (31.1035g), not standard ounces. When converting Nepal gold prices (per Tola) to international prices (per troy oz), use: 1 Troy Oz = 2.6667 Tola.
